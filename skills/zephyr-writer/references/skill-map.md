# Dragon Novel Style Skill Map

## 1. Daily-life ballast
Use clutter, errands, messages, bills, devices, food, transport, weather, family nagging, or workplace drudgery as ballast.
Purpose: make later impossibility feel earned.

## 2. Petty protagonist buffer
Let the protagonist convert pain into something smaller:
- a joke
- a shopping calculation
- a side complaint
- a mean little comparison
- a low-grade fantasy

Purpose: avoid clean melodrama.

## 3. Humiliation conversion
Convert “I got hurt” into:
- “this is ridiculous”
- “if X happened to my character...”
- “fine, whatever”
Then let the actual hurt leak later.

## 4. Concrete delivery of fate
Deliver turning points through something physical:
- letter
- email
- broken object
- package
- system notification
- account ban
- receipt
- train ticket

Purpose: keep extraordinary events tactile.

## 5. Delayed grandeur
Do not announce destiny too early.
Keep the narrative in low-ceiling reality until the last workable moment.

## 6. Shared-task intimacy
Build relationship through doing things together:
- walking someone somewhere
- lending clothes or gear
- repairing equipment
- escorting, carrying, stabilizing
- splitting labor in action scenes
- post-battle cleanup, travel, waiting, eating, seasickness, weather

Purpose: make affection emerge through competence and presence, not early confession.

## 7. Hard mouth, soft hands
Let a character sound impatient, clipped, or mildly rude while their actions clearly reveal care.

Pattern:
1. complain or tease
2. still help
3. refuse to name it tenderness

## 8. Tactical chemistry
In action or logistics scenes, reveal intimacy through division of labor.
One character may strike, the other may assess, repair, warn, coordinate, or translate.
This creates closeness without requiring overt sentiment.

## 9. Indirect memory return
When recovering a bond or lost feeling, avoid immediate explanation.
Let memory return through:
- familiar phrasing
- repeated caretaking
- bodily habits
- food or drink preferences
- practical routines that feel too natural to be accidental

## 10. Character-perspective narration (新增)
**Core principle:** Show the world through the character's eyes, not the author's explanation.

**Key techniques:**
- Replace "not A, but B" with "A? No. B? Not really."
- Use short sentences and questions to mimic thought process
- Show concrete actions instead of abstract emotions
- Let readers discover alongside the character

**Examples:**
- Instead of "He wasn't angry, just disappointed" → "Angry? Maybe. But more like... tired. Like he'd been holding something heavy for too long."
- Instead of "She realized he might be lying" → "The timeline didn't add up. Tuesday he said he was home, but the receipt in his pocket was dated Tuesday, from a cafe across town."

**Purpose:** Create immersion by making readers experience the story through the character's senses and thoughts, not through authorial commentary.
